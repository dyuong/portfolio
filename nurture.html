Nurture
Revolutionalising plant care
The Problem
Plant owners often struggle to look after their plants - whether it being how often to water it or what to do about diseases and fungi.
The Solution
Nurture is a mobile app which allows users to scan and identify plants, learn about what conditions are most suitable for their plant, and set up reminders to water and fertilise their plants - all in one!

Research
Our group hypothesised that since COVID, an increasing number of plant-growers are struggling to keep their plants healthy in their households. We experienced this personally as a group, and were interested to see if this was the case amongst other people as well.

Our group utilised both qualitative and quantitative research methods to try and determine this, with the summary of our research displayed below.
Interviews
Our group completed 6 one-to-one interviews with plant owners.

We categorized the data through an affinity diagram to get a better picture of the data collected.
Surveys
We also sent out a survey which was completed by 21 respondents. Some of the key findings are displayed on the graphs below.

9 out of 21 respondents (43%) reports themselves as average at looking after their plants, with 8 out of 21 respondents (39%) reporting themselves as poor or very poor.

10 out of 21 respondents (47%) report themselves as having poor or very poor knowledge in the conditions best suited for their plants.

14 out of 21 respondents (67%) report they would use a plant identification scanning app.

20 out of 21 respondents (95%) report they would benefit from added guidance in looking after their plant

Define
Analysing the data, a couple of key insights came across:
A large percentage of plant owners struggle to look after their plants

People struggle to look after their plants for a number of different reasons (eg. watering frequency, pests and diseases, humidity etc.)
Keeping the insights learnt in mind, our team developed a user persona to better represent our target users, as well as help guide the rest of our design process.
Primary Persona: The Struggling Plant Owner
Our group also made a user journey map to describe the current journey plant owners may experience when looking after their plants. Developing the journey map allowed us further insight into pain points users may experience, which we kept in mind for later on in the design process.
Problem Statement
Lastly, we defined a problem statement which our app would be designed to solve.
“How might we help plant owners who are struggling to look after their indoor plants be more knowledgeable about the conditions best suited for their plants, so that their plants are healthier and less likely to die.”
Ideate
After defining our user persona and problem statement, it was time to generate ideas for our plant care mobile app.

Our first step was to perform a competitor analysis of similar apps on the market. This gave us an inspiration for our eventual designs, whilst also alowing us to better understand the current market landscape and potential gaps we could fill.
After completing our competitor analysis, we realised that the market was already quite saturated with similar concepts of a plant-care app. Given our limited time-frame, rather than pivoting to a different idea or concept, we decided to focus on designing the key features users would need really well, whilst trying to improve on some of the weaknesses we noticed in other apps on the market.
Finally, we constructed user flows for the main features we planned to design.
Prototype & Testing
After deciding on the features for our mobile app, it was finally time to start building our designs. We opened up Figma and started building our low fidelity wireframes, before refining them to a higher fidelity for usability testing.

High Fidelity Wireframes
After creating our higher fidelity wireframes, we conducted 8 usability tests to validate our ideas. Users were asked to work through the 3 main user flows we had designed, commenting on any pain points that they encountered. Some of the key feedback is displayed below:
Key Feedback
  • User's found adding a watering reminder long winded and confusing
  • Unable to add watering reminder from the plant details page
  • Unable to edit last watering date for a specific plant
    • Overall the feedback was quite positive, and we only made minor adjustments in building our final clickable prototype. We also created a UI style guide to ensure our design stayed consisted across the interface.
