Research
Hypothesised Problem
With the idea of developing a travel itinerary app, I hypothesised that people have difficulty documenting and keeping track of their travel itinerary. I also hypthesised that people want an easier way to collaborate on their itinerary, without having to constantly meet up online with their fellow travellers.

I set out to test my hypotheses, and utilised both qualitative and quantitative research methods to find out more about the pain points people experienced when planning their travel.
Interviews
I conducted 6 one-to-one interviews with people who have traveled in the past. The objective of the interview was to understand the pain points people experienced when planning their trips.

I collated the data onto sticky notes, and then categorized the data through an affinity diagram to get a better picture of the data collected.
Surveys
I also conducted a survey which was completed by 17 participants. Some of the key findings are displayed below.

6 out of 17 respondents (35%) find it difficult to collaborate with other people when planning their trip

6 out of 17 respondents (35%) find it difficult to document and keep track of their itinerary.

Majority of respondents would prefer to work on the itinerary in their own time than in a group

14 out of 17 respondents (82%) use Google Sheets/Google Docs to plan their itinerary

Define
After looking at the research, a few key insights came across:
User Insights
People prefer to work on the itinerary in their own time

People find it difficult to keep track of changes to their itinerary

People find it stressful when changes are made to the itinerary
The insights and data generally supported my hypothesis - people found keeping track of the itinerary could be difficult (especially when there were changes), and that people found it easier to work on the itinerary in their own time. It also showcased how different people were when it came to planning; some people were extremely detailed and structured, whilst others were spontaneous and flexible.
User Persona
From the research, I was also able to define a user persona to represent my target audience.
User Journey Map
And depict a typical journey that my target user would undertake.
Lastly, I defined two main problems that my mobile app was to try and solve:
Problem Statement
How might we ensure that users can easily document and keep track of changes to their itinerary?

How might we allow for a collaborative planning method which allows users to work on the itinerary in their own time?
Ideate
After defining personas and a problem statement, it was time to generate ideas for my itinerary planning mobile app.
Competitor Analysis
I started by completing a competitor analysis with other itinerary-planning apps in the market, which allowed me to determine any market gaps to capitalise on. This step also served as an inspiration for features for my app to include, as well as the overall look and feel of my eventual design.
User Flows
Lastly, I created some user flows for key features my mobile app was to include, based off the insights I had gained through my research. This step also served as a basis for my initial wireframes.
Prototype & Testing
After ideating, it was finally time to start building my designs. Eager to create, I opened up Figma and began working on my initial wireframes.
Low Fidelity Wireframes
Usability Tests (Low Fidelity)
After designing my initial wireframes, it was time to do some usability testing to help validate my concepts. Users were asked to work through the main features of the app, and comment on any pain points that they encountered.

I completed 6 usability tests in total, with some of the key feedback being displayed below:
Key Feedback
  • User's found it difficult to tell the difference between actual and proposed events
  • User's found it difficult to add a collaborator to the itinerary
  • Unable to see length of each item on the itinerary
  • Icons a bit overwhelming on the home page
    • Considering the feedback, I iterated and refined my designs, working towards a high fidelity prototype.

    Reflections
    Importance of Usability Testing
    Ideally undergoing more extensive usability testing throughout the design process would have been ideal, but due to time limitations I wasnâ€™t able to complete as much usability testing between iterations as I would have liked.

    Throughout this challenge, usability testing provided key feedback which guided future iterations - it really emphasised the importance or testing with potentially real users to keep the concept and designs aligned with user needs and expectations.
    Expectations and Prioritisation
    Being a 3 week individual design challenge meant that I had to set realistic expectations of what I was going to try and achieve from the outset.

    Throughout this challenge I quickly learnt the importance of prioritising with regards to research methods, features I wanted to implement, and the overall look and feel of the high fidelity prototype.

    It also required me to think about what was really important to users, and to prioritise working on these things first.
